Legend — How to Read This Document
Keyword Metrics
KDKeyword Difficulty (Ahrefs, 0–100). Measures how many referring domains you need to rank in the top 10. Lower = easier to rank.
KD Scale
0–14 Easy15–29 Medium30–49 Hard50+ Very Hard
SVSearch Volume — estimated monthly US searches from Ahrefs. The number of times this exact keyword is searched per month.
TPTraffic Potential — estimated total monthly visits you could receive if ranked #1, accounting for the full keyword cluster (not just the exact match). Often much larger than SV.
CPCCost Per Click — what advertisers pay per click on Google Ads. High CPC signals strong commercial intent: the visitor is ready to spend money.
Parent TopicThe broader keyword cluster this term belongs to in Ahrefs. Ranking for the parent topic means you likely rank for the child keyword too.
Competitive Intelligence
DRDomain Rating (Ahrefs, 0–100). Measures the overall backlink authority of a website. A page at DR 25 is much easier to outrank than one at DR 85.
DR Scale
0–39 Beatable40–69 Moderate70–100 Authority
Beatable CompThe lowest-DR site currently ranking in the top 10. This is your first target — build a better page than theirs and earn more links.
Avg Competitor DRMean DR of all non-outlier results in the top 10. Gives a realistic read on how competitive the SERP is overall. Outliers (gov, mega-brands) are excluded.
OutlierA result ranking due to brand authority (irs.gov, legalzoom, reddit) rather than content quality. They are essentially impossible to outrank — build around them, not against them.
Word Count TargetRecommended content length based on what is currently ranking for each keyword. Match or exceed the beatable competitor's word count.
SERP Features
Local Pack
Google shows a map + 3 local business listings at the top. Strong signal of geo-intent. Optimize Google Business Profile alongside the page.
AI Overview
Google's AI-generated summary appears above organic results. Informational keywords trigger this. Writing concise, authoritative answers increases inclusion chances.
PAA
People Also Ask — expandable question boxes. Each PAA question is a standalone blog post opportunity. Answering them directly on the landing page also increases ranking signals.
Top Stories
Google News carousel. Signals the keyword is trending or news-adjacent. Not a primary target for evergreen pages — focus on organic rankings.
Content Format Signals
Service PageGoogle is currently rewarding attorney/service pages for this keyword. Write the page as a service description + credentials + CTA. Not a blog post.
Article · How-toGoogle wants a step-by-step guide or educational explanation. Use headers, numbered lists, clear structure. 1,200–2,500 words typically.
Article · ComparisonGoogle is surfacing head-to-head comparison content (e.g. LLC vs S-Corp). Use a structured compare/contrast format. Tables work well here.
Article · ListicleGoogle is ranking list-format content (e.g. "8 legal documents every startup needs"). Numbered format, each item with a brief explanation.
Article · ChecklistGoogle wants a downloadable or scannable checklist format. Short, action-oriented items. Great for lead magnets.
Content Tags
PAA
People Also Ask — this post directly answers a question Ahrefs captured in the SERP. Prioritize these; they have a clear, proven search intent.
KD 0
Keyword Difficulty is zero — no backlinks required to rank. These are the fastest wins. Publish good content and you can rank within days to weeks.
Pillar
Long-form cornerstone content (2,500+ words) that covers a broad topic and links out to multiple supporting posts. Builds topical authority for the entire Land.
geo
Geo-targeted post — targets a specific city or state. Lower competition than national terms. Signals local relevance to Google.
Page Tiers & Priority
Tier ABuild first. KD 0–5 with strong commercial intent. These pages should be live before any blog content is published. Low effort, high return.
Tier BBuild next. KD 6–20. Strong long-term value. Will take longer to rank but worth the investment once Tier A pages are established.
Sub-PageA dedicated landing page for a single keyword — not a blog post. Gets its own URL, full service page treatment, and inbound links from the parent Land page.
Internal LinkingEvery blog post links back to its parent landing page. Every landing page links to its supporting blog posts. Cross-Land links follow the map on this document. All pages end with a paid intake CTA.
CTA RuleNo free consults. Every page ends with an email CTA to book a paid intake. No DIY instructions — give awareness, not execution.

Recurring Competitor Weaknesses — Delina's Marketing Angles
Five structural patterns appeared across virtually every competitor in the research. These are not random oversights — they are the predictable consequences of how most law firms and legal platforms are built. Each one is a marketing angle Delina can directly exploit in content, positioning, and messaging.
01
The Free Consult Trap
Who does this: Nearly every boutique in the competitive set — dolanfamilylaw.com, Thya Law, ESQgo, Mollaei Law, most California family law and business attorneys. It is the default model for attorney client acquisition.
Delina's Angle
The free consult signals low value before a word is spoken. It attracts price-shoppers, not serious clients. Delina's paid intake model communicates premium positioning from the first touchpoint — and every piece of content can explain why free consults are actually a red flag for the high-earner who values their own time.
02
Opaque Pricing Breeds Distrust
Who does this: BigLaw universally. Most boutiques — NEO Law Group, Thya Law, Wilson Tax Law, Charitable Allies, ESQgo. Even national brands like Mark Kohler and Anderson Advisors hide firm rates behind inquiry forms.
Delina's Angle
The $250K+ earner has a sophisticated BS detector. Opaque pricing signals the firm is either embarrassed by their rates or expects to negotiate. Transparent flat-fee positioning removes friction, builds trust before the first conversation, and attracts clients who already respect the value — not clients who will haggle.
03
Generalist Positioning Attracts the Wrong Clients
Who does this: Family law firms covering prenups alongside divorce. General business attorneys covering contracts, LLC, and "whatever you need." ContractsCounsel and UpCounsel marketplace attorneys who handle anything. LegalShield network attorneys.
Delina's Angle
Generalist positioning signals average expertise. A creator earning $800K/year who needs a prenup, an S-corp election, trademark protection, and brand deal contracts does not want to hire four different generalist attorneys. They want the specialist who already understands their world. Every piece of Delina's content reinforces the niche: this is built for you specifically.
04
Single-Lane Focus Fragments the Client Relationship
Who does this: Thya Law (contracts and talent only), Trademark Engine (filing only), ESQgo (Amazon disputes only), Wilson Tax Law (IRS disputes only), Clerky (Delaware C-corp docs only), Collective (S-corp + bookkeeping only).
Delina's Angle
Every single-lane competitor forces their client to maintain multiple attorney relationships for what is fundamentally one integrated problem: protecting wealth they built in an unconventional way. Delina's multi-land practice means one attorney, one intake, one relationship that sees the whole picture. This is both a business development advantage and a core content theme — the total cost of fragmented legal counsel.
05
Platforms Sell Documents, Not Understanding
Who does this: LegalZoom, ZenBusiness, Bizee, HelloPrenup, The Contract Shop, Rocket Lawyer, LegalShield. The entire Tier 1–3 pricing stack. Collectively responsible for millions of inadequately protected founders and creators who believe they are legally covered.
Delina's Angle
The platform client gets a document but doesn't understand what it does, what it doesn't cover, or whether it will hold up. Delina's content strategy can directly surface this gap — not to fear-monger, but to show the difference between having a contract and having a strategy. Every blog post that answers "do I need a lawyer for X" is a top-of-funnel entry point from clients who started with a platform and are starting to suspect they need more.
